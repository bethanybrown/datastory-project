Privacy on Google

What does Google do with the data that it collects?

There are over 40,000 Google searches processed every second, making over 3.5 billion searches per day and 1.2 trillion searches per year worldwide. Surprisingly, Google collects all of the data that it can from keeping what you search on record to taking note of where you are. The data that Google collects contributes to advancing all aspects of Google's outreach. Even when you use Google Maps, your destination and tracked journey lead to creating a more user friendly experience for everyone. Your travels help to confirm and declare the timing of trips and traffic, and this is only possibly through data collection of every user. However, the deeper you dig into how much Google knows about you based on daily activities, the more people are concerned with their privacy. As long as you have a Google account, which most people do, they have access to your name, birthday, email, social accounts, calendar, and other personal information. In order to ensure consumers' privacy, Google breaks down their policies on their privacy pages. More information can be found in the Resources.
